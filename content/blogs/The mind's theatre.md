---
title : The Mind's Theatre
date : 2026-04-04T19:54:37+05:30
tags : ['Philosophy','Life']
---

Humans are interesting creatures. They believe in so many things. They believe they have to do so many things.
They believe so much depend on them.

They fight battles in their own minds for an audience that exists only in their heads.
How much ever they fight, however many victories they gain, they always find a new hurdle, a new enemy. The reason these enemies gather in their minds is because of this audience.
They become a gladiator in a colosseum filled with people who judge them, some real, some not.

If one is truly pained by this occurrence, the way to stop it is not to cycle through the multiple ways in which you can dominate your mental enemy. In fact, the enemy exists only to be dominated and defeated. As long as you keep defeating it, you feed it. You keep it's existence alive. The way to stop it is to kill the audience. Kill the spectators, not the gladiator. The audience in your mind- why do they exist ? For many people, they are their parents who wanted complete control over their lives, for others they are teachers who had delusional expectations of omnipotence and omniscience.

It is not possible to live your life constantly battling for an audience that would never be satisfied.
You will wear yourself out. And then they will still torment you.
This can be seen in many places. Even in the most unexpected places.
There are people who wish to create music or art, but they do it solely to please others, to show them that they can do something, and they always imagine themselves performing the best performance they've done, only for it to never be enough. There are people who look at the unique things that other people have achieved over a lifetime and constantly dream of having achieved it in their teenage years, just to get the approval of their teachers and peers. It is very sad indeed. Some people are not even capable of doing these things, and only imagining. Not because they are not physically able to , but because their existence is derived from fantasizing about doing it, that they never actually do it. Also, to them, it appears impossible to do, so everything that they see in front of them, they fantasize about being the ones to do it, and show it off to the audience in their heads.

If the reason for you watching accomplished people or listening to loud music for tens of hours on end is because of this, please stop. Deal with the real enemy instead of fantasizing about overcoming the assignments the audience gives you.
