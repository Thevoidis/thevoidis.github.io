title : Jobs again
---

It's been a long time now since I started looking for jobs.
It doesn't feel like I'll get one anytime soon either.
This life sucks, really. Having no money is one thing, but being degraded for not having a job is another.
It's not like I'm actively trying to sabotage my chances at a job. It's that I almost never get one.
When I tell my parents that one of my friends got a temporary underpaying job (which they probably got after a few dozen rejections while also being more qualified than me), they have the balls to point to that very example and says "See, everyone is taking care of their future. You are the only one who will be left behind."

As if I'm not trying. As if I'm some kind of prick. I will be honest. I do not want to work a job. I don't think anyone wants to work a job. All the people working, if you just tell them that they'll get their salaries and perks even without working , and make work optional, I would assume that they would not show up for work from the next day. Because even if you really like doing something, working on that as a job under bosses and clients who you hate and give you contracts you don't like drains the passion from you. 

I don't know what to do. I could do freelancing, but it's not that easy either. You actually need to keep doing something well for years even to be noticed. And even then there would be many that are better than you at it. I don't think I can keep such things up man. I don't even have a will to live, forget one to do all this.

I think that the system that the world was set up with is kind of breaking. Especially in countries like mine, which never really developed anything. They skipped directly from the agricultural sector to the service sector ignoring the fact that you have to create something to provide services on top of them. For most Indians until now, the escape hatch was to go to other countries. But that is not going to work anymore because these "other countries" themselves are facing unemployment crises. Indians have nowhere to run anymore. The government's upside down policies so far are pretty bad. They need to focus more on actual manufacturing and processing and infrastructure for all that rather than generating more doctors and engineers.

The rest of the world is not that much better off either. The system where you keep cutting jobs as technology improves is causing a lot of instability among the middle class in most western countries. The work that could be done by ten people now can be done by one person with an AI subscription. And of course, even this person is not likely to get a raise. This does seem to benefit companies in the short run, but I genuinely don't understand how you can have an economy where no one has an income.

Sure, the rich do have an income, but you can't sell a billion tons of bread to the rich alone. If the rich want to keep getting richer, they should then concentrate on making services for other rich people, because now they have all the money. So for the middle class, there are no jobs and no services. There is no safety net for these people under the current economic systems. If the systems were to change, then maybe there would be, but as of right now, I see no change on the horizon.
Do billions of middle class just get demoted to the new impoverished class ? Do the existing poor people just starve ? Assuming that the system won't change , is there any hope left even in the current system ?
