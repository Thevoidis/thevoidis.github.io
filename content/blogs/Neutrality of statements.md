I have come to realize that most things that humans say amounts to nothing.
Even statements that are objectively true need not "add" anything.
Let's say you're having a conversation. If someone says "You can see the sun during the day." It doesn't add anything to the conversation.
If you're having a debate about economic systems, saying "You're a communist !" has nothing to do with the conversation (whether it be true or not).
If someone tries to defame some famous person, maybe a fan of that famous person can say "but he earns more than you." (which does not add anything to the conversation, but might still offend the defamer for some reason). The defamer can also respond with something like "You're the kind of person to flex other people's money." ( Which also adds nothing to the conversation and can offend the fan for some reason.)

The common theme in the examples above is that human "conversations" are based on emotions and responses, not on objectivity.
People speak and respond to incite emotions and expressions, not to state or question anything valid.
Therefore, most human speech is neutral. The emotions within them is intentional, but ultimately pointless.
If these statements are neutral, then why would one get excited over them ? Why would anyone get offended over them ?
It is most likely the human brain responding to threats and treats, not rational choice.

When one's actions are based on emotion and not logic, it puts one in a difficult position of being a slave to one's own and others' emotions and words and actions.
For such a person, their entire model of the world falls apart every time something something unpredicted happens. Every they get hurt, it feels like the world is falling apart. Every time they get happier, it feels like the world is a better place.

Of course, I am not saying that "The world" should be anyone's primary focus of attention or concern. I am simply stating what such an emotional person feels.
My question is, if a stranger acted like the world depended on their whims, why should you pay attention to what this stranger has to say about the world ? To them, the world revolves around their emotions. It is a private place. And you are a part of that "world" of theirs, so according to them, you too revolve around them. You are in their private place. Would you pay heed to such things ? Would you agree with such concepts ? If not , then why do you feel offended or excited by mere words by these people ? The fact that you take them personally makes you acknowledge that your mind lives in the "worlds" of these people, that they revolve around too many things that are not worth revolving around.
